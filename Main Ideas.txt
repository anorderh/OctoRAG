Main Ideas

Boards - Collection of content itself
Finds - Resources
Framing - Aesthetic or useful organization of finds

* You have boards and finds. Boards contain finds and finds are links pulled form internet w/ hyperlink, thumbnail, short blurb etc.
* Finds can be from any online resource, but this will identified. Most common groupings are youtube, twitter, wikipedia, pinterest, tiktok, instagram, etc.
	- Pages can be directly linked
* Boards have 2 options for positoning content
	- Static (blocks which can be assigned column-like lik Trello boards & supports internal directories)
	- Dynamic (blocks can be placed around in random positions, so longer as they're not intersecting)
* Finds can be repositoned within the board and said positioning will be saved
* To better organize and/or customize boards, Framing is introduced
	- Graphical tools, images, text boxes... static content to better understand a board's purpose, organization, and/or appeasement
* Tags are appended to boards & resources, based on their content. Can be manually added as well
* User tracking
	- Pins --> Tags
	- Follow --> Boards, other users
	- Following pulls content into feed, whereas tags can be saved into a users' feed to search specifically for boards and/or content with the mentioned tag

Displaying boards
	Instead of having boards be fully customizable w/ positoning and images, have boards be represented as Split Packed Bubble Charts, similar to https://www.highcharts.com/demo/highcharts/packed-bubble-split
	Organization can be consequently simplified implementation wise
		- Finds within a board can be given an "importance level"
			- Higher importance means larger bubble
			- Higher importance means closer to center
		- Finds can also be given "groupings", str[]
			- Each element in array is level of organization
			- Groups together related findings, but client will also allow subgroupings to be emphasized
